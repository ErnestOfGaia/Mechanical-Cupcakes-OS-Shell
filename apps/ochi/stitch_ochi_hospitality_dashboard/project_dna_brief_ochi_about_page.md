# Project DNA Brief: OCHI "About" Page

## 1. Project Overview
**Product Name:** OCHI (Oregon Coast Hospitality Index)
**Concept:** A predictive hospitality framework that translates complex environmental and economic data into actionable operational intelligence for the Oregon Coast region.
**Core Mission:** To empower hospitality stakeholders with high-fidelity volume forecasts, enabling better labor allocation and inventory management.

## 2. Visual Identity & Tone
**Style:** Minimalist, high-contrast, data-centric.
**Vibe:** Professional, utility-driven, "Technical Chic."
**Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter/Roboto), varying weights to establish hierarchy.
**Palette:** Monochrome base (White/Black/Grey) with functional status colors (Success Green, Warning Amber, Alert Red).
**No-Fly Zone:** Strictly no avian or "owl" themed branding. No decorative fluff.

## 3. Screen Purpose: The "About" Page
This page is the educational bridge between the raw data and the user's understanding. It must explain *how* OCHI works without getting bogged down in math.

### Key Sections:
1. **The Vision:** Brief statement on why OCHI exists (the forecasting gap in coastal hospitality).
2. **The "Data Five" Pillars:** A deep dive into the five core metrics:
    - **Hwy 6 Status:** Real-time traffic fluidity from the primary arterial pulse (Hwy 6). 90-minute lead time on arrivals.
    - **Gas Index:** Regional fuel volatility. High prices = "Inertia Coefficient" (fewer day-trippers).
    - **Search Interest:** Aggregated digital intent for coastal keywords.
    - **TLT Pulse:** Transient Lodging Tax collections. The foundational economic baseline.
    - **OCHI Forecast:** The synthesized output. Meteorological weightings (Visual Utility vs. Temperature).
3. **The Logic (Simplified):** Explain that OCHI isn't just a weather app; it's a weighted algorithm that adjusts based on seasonal variance.
4. **Target Audience:** General managers, HR leads (for staffing), and procurement officers.

## 4. Design Requirements
- **Mobile-First Navigation:** Text-based links (Dashboard | About | Contact).
- **Hierarchy:** Large, bold headings followed by concise, digestible body text.
- **Iconography:** Minimal, functional icons only if they clarify the data type.
- **CTA:** At the bottom, a simple link to "View Live Dashboard."
